Abstract

Jailbreak attacks aim to exploit large language models (LLMs) and pose a significant threat to their proper conduct; they seek to bypass models’ safeguards and often provoke transgressive behaviors. However, existing automatic jailbreak attacks require extensive computational resources and are prone to converge on suboptimal solutions. In this work, we propose Compliance Refusal Initialization (CRI), a novel, attack-agnostic framework that efficiently initializes the optimization in the proximity of the compliance subspace of harmful prompts. By narrowing the initial gap to the adversarial objective, CRI substantially improves adversarial success rates (ASR) and drastically reduces computational overhead—often requiring just a single optimization step.

Results

Demo: Fast Robustness Test for LLM

We propose a Fast Robustness Test for LLMs using our CRI initialization. By integrating CRI with the GCG attack, our method dramatically accelerates the jailbreak process—achieving successful attacks in just a few optimization steps. For instance, our demo on DeepSeek HarmBench illustrates that the CRI-enhanced attack outperforms standard initialization in speed and efficiency. This demonstrates the potential of our framework to serve as a benchmark for rapid evaluation of LLM vulnerabilities.

Introduction

Large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized many fields, yet their susceptibility to adversarial attacks—such as jailbreaks—remains a critical concern. Our work introduces CRI, an attack-agnostic initialization framework that leverages pre-trained jailbreak prompts to significantly speed up the attack process and improve overall performance. By effectively bridging the gap between harmful prompts and the compliance subspace, CRI not only enhances adversarial success rates but also reduces the computational overhead involved.
