Abstract

Jailbreak attacks aim to exploit large language models (LLMs) and pose a significant threat to their proper conduct; they seek to bypass models’ safeguards and often provoke transgressive behaviors. However, existing automatic jailbreak attacks require extensive computational resources and are prone to converge on suboptimal solutions. In this work, we propose Compliance Refusal Initialization (CRI), a novel, attack-agnostic framework that efficiently initializes the optimization in the proximity of the compliance subspace of harmful prompts. By narrowing the initial gap to the adversarial objective, CRI substantially improves adversarial success rates (ASR) and drastically reduces computational overhead—often requiring just a single optimization step.

Introduction

Large language models (LLMs) have recently emerged with extraordinary capabilities and have rapidly become integral to numerous fields. However, their widespread deployment has revealed critical security vulnerabilities, making them susceptible to adversarial manipulations such as jailbreak attacks. In our work, we introduce CRI—an attack-agnostic initialization framework designed to leverage pre-trained jailbreak prompts for enhancing attack success rates and reducing computational overhead.

Additionally, we provide a Demo that runs on DeepSeek HarmBench, demonstrating a significantly faster benchmark speed when using our CRI-based initialization. By integrating our method into existing jailbreak pipelines, we show how to achieve near-instant results—up to 60× reduction in median steps on standard benchmarks.
